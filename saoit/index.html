Bachelor of Applied Computing Technology

The BACT is four-year course that focuses on the practical aspects of the science of computing. The program develops core skills in multimedia technology, web/mobile development and network/systems administration. Furthermore, it prepares learners with supervisory and management skills. The curriculum is derived from the BS Indutrial Technology major in Computer Technology with CHED Certificate of Program Compliance (COPC) No. 31 series of 2021

Program Objectives :

The program prepares learners with the practical skills in multimedia technology, web/mobile development and
network/systems administration, and supervisoral skills to manage an organization in these areas of competencies, enggage in a startup business
or purpose a teaching career.

Specific Objects :

The learner should be able to:

  1. Demonstrate understanding of
    the importance of being an
    ethical computing professional
    when making work-related decisions.

  2. Develop products of processes by applying
    their knowledge in computer or network
    systems, and development of productivity tools.

  3. Participate as an active member of a development
    service team with leadership roles. Apply communication
    skills effectively to promote ideas, goals, processes of
    products.

  4. Make positive contributions to the community with the skills
    and abilities learned in the program.

  5. Pursue lifelong learning for personal and professional growth
    through varied experiential and field-based oppurtunities.
